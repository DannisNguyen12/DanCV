About Trey Ratcliff

Trey Ratcliff was born on July 7, 1971, in Austin, Texas. Despite being born blind in one eye, he developed a unique vision that would define his extraordinary career in photography. After studying Computer Science at Southern Methodist University, he combined his technical foundation with artistic sensibilities to pioneer innovative approaches to digital photography.

In 2005, he founded his photography blog "Stuck in Customs," which quickly gained popularity and established him as a leading voice in travel and HDR photography. His photograph "Fourth at Lake Austin" became the first HDR photograph ever to be displayed at the prestigious Smithsonian Museum in Washington D.C.

In 2012, Ratcliff relocated to Queenstown, New Zealand, where he currently resides. His photographs have been viewed more than 150 billion times via Google-based channels, and he has amassed over 16 million followers across his social media accounts, making him one of the most viewed photographers in the digital age.

HDR Photography Techniques

Trey Ratcliff is widely regarded as a pioneer and master of High Dynamic Range (HDR) photography

Multiple Exposure Capture

Capturing 3-7 bracketed shots at different exposure values to capture both the darkest shadows and the brightest highlights in a scene.

Tone Mapping

Using sophisticated tone mapping techniques to combine multiple exposures into a single image that preserves detail across the entire dynamic range.

Vibrant Color Palette

Creating images with vibrant, saturated colors that evoke emotional responses rather than pursuing strict realism.

Digital Processing Workflow

A detailed workflow combining Adobe Lightroom for initial adjustments with specialized HDR software for tone mapping and final adjustments.
